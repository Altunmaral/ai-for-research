How We Use Claude

Seven ways Claude powers
Etch research

Claude has become embedded across the full research lifecycle at Etch - not as a replacement for researcher judgment, but as the engine that handles heavy lifting so researchers can focus on what matters: reading between the lines.

01 - Analysis & Synthesis

Turning transcripts into insight documents

After every research session, transcripts are uploaded and Claude generates a first-pass findings document. It quantifies recurring themes across sessions, surfaces patterns, and structures raw data into a readable artefact. The team then reviews, challenges, and adds the insights only a researcher could catch.

Core Workflow
02 - Prototyping

Building solutions directly from research transcripts

After general employee research, transcripts were fed into Claude to generate solution concepts and insights for SAYE and SIP prototypes. This was later extended to Share Awards following the Discovery workshop with SME feedback. Research-to-prototype in hours, not weeks.

High Impact
03 - Synthetic Users

Fast, agile validation before recruitment begins

At the start of a project or straight after a prototype is built, we use AI-simulated user profiles to run a quick, low-cost validation round. This gives us early directional signal before we commit to a full recruitment cycle - accelerating the work, reducing cost at the exploratory stage, and making the real research sessions sharper when they happen.

Agile Testing
04 - Personas & User Journeys

From raw data to visual research artefacts

Claude generates structured user personas and detailed journey maps from research transcripts and synthesis documents. These become the shared language between design, product, and stakeholders - grounded in evidence rather than assumption.

Research Artefacts
05 - SME Feedback

Comment sections and visualised meeting summaries

When sharing prototypes, flows, or visualised meeting summaries with stakeholders, Claude generates exportable comment sections for structured SME input. After the Discretionary Discovery Meeting, meeting notes were visualised to sharpen understanding before the live research sessions began.

Stakeholder Engagement
06 - Research Ops Portal

A living archive of all EQ research

A Research Operations Portal is being built to host all EQ research chronologically - videos, transcripts, findings, prototype links, and decisions in one place. Including Lyssna outputs. The goal is institutional memory that doesn't get lost after a project ends.

In Progress
07 - Client Correspondence

Email triage and admin tracking

Parsing and following up on Client Admin correspondence. Claude helps organise email threads, draft responses, and maintain a tracking system for ongoing client admin loops - reducing time spent on coordination and surfacing what actually needs attention.

Deeper Look

Analysis & synthesis
in practice

The synthesis workflow is where Claude delivers the most measurable time savings - but also where the limits of AI are most visible.

01

Upload session transcripts

Raw transcripts from research sessions (or Lyssna exports) are uploaded directly. Claude reads across multiple sessions simultaneously - something that takes a researcher hours to do manually.

02

Generate first-pass findings

Claude produces a structured document: recurring themes, frequency counts, participant quotes, and a prioritised list of insights. This draft would otherwise take a full day to produce manually.

03

Researcher review and refinement

The team reads through the AI output and - critically - adds the insights that only come from being in the room. The nuance, the hesitation in someone's voice, the thing that wasn't said but clearly meant. These are the findings that matter most and that Claude consistently misses.

04

Finalise and share

The refined document becomes the single source of truth for a research cycle. Insights feed directly into prototyping, requirements, and stakeholder communications.

Key Learning: Claude is excellent at quantifying what appeared in the data. It reliably misses what the data implies. The most important insights in recent sessions - including the standout finding from the Discretionary and Executive research - had to be added manually by the researcher after review. Human judgment remains the irreplaceable layer.
Agile Testing

Synthetic users
built from real data

Synthetic users are not a replacement for real recruitment - they're what happens before it. A fast, low-cost validation layer that sharpens direction and makes the live research sessions more focused when they do happen.

When Used

At project kick-off

Before any recruitment begins, synthetic users give early directional signal on concepts. They help us test assumptions quickly and identify where the real research needs to focus.

When Used

Straight after a prototype is built

Once a prototype is ready, synthetic users provide immediate feedback before a recruitment round is commissioned - catching obvious friction and refining the design before live participants see it.

When Used

Between research cycles

When a new question emerges mid-project and there is no time to recruit, synthetic users bridge the gap - keeping momentum without the wait.

Built From

Real research transcripts

Synthetic users are grounded in data from previous live sessions, making them contextually accurate for EQ's specific scheme types and admin profiles.

Honest Assessment

What works, and
what doesn't

An honest audit. Claude is genuinely transformative for some aspects of research operations - and genuinely limited in others. Understanding the boundary is what makes it useful.

What Claude Does Well

Quantifying research output

Counting how many times an insight appeared across sessions. Surfacing patterns. Building frequency tables. Reliable and fast.

Prototyping at speed

Turning research transcripts into interactive concepts, flows, and visualisations faster than any traditional workflow.

Organising and structuring

Affinity mapping, journey mapping, persona structuring - Claude handles the scaffolding so researchers can focus on judgment calls.

Visual artefact generation

Personas, journey maps, meeting visualisations, and insight clusters that communicate research findings clearly to stakeholders.

Correspondence at scale

Managing and triaging bulk client admin emails that would otherwise consume significant researcher bandwidth.

Where Claude Falls Short

Reading between the lines

The most important insights from recent sessions - including the standout finding from the Discretionary and Executive research - were not in Claude's top findings. They required a researcher to be in the room.

Missing what was implied, not said

Hesitation, body language, the thing a participant almost said but didn't - Claude processes words, not meaning. Human researchers process both.

Prioritising by importance, not frequency

Claude ranks by occurrence. Researchers rank by significance. These are not the same thing. A finding mentioned once by the right person can be more important than one mentioned ten times by others.

Context and stakes

Claude does not know the political history of a client relationship, the operational constraints of a scheme, or why a particular finding will land differently in a particular room. Researchers do.

The principle that holds: AI accelerates. Humans decide. Always. Claude does the heavy lifting. Researchers do the thinking.
Future Thinking

What comes
next

Now that Claude knows the full shape of how Etch does research - the schemes, the participants, the workflows, the decisions - it can start contributing to strategy, not just execution.

01 - Strategy

Research strategy recommendations

Claude knows what we have researched, what gaps exist, and what decisions are still unsupported by evidence. It can now proactively suggest what to research next, which findings need validation, and where the biggest unknown risks are in the current roadmap.

02 - Recruitment

Build our own recruitment tool

Instead of paying for third-party recruitment platforms, we could build our own participant management system - screener design, outreach templates, scheduling, incentive tracking. Claude can generate the infrastructure; researchers manage the relationships.

03 - Testing Platform

An internal Lyssna alternative

Build lightweight usability testing flows directly within our own system rather than depending on external platforms. Prototype testing, task analysis, and preference testing - all hosted, controlled, and connected to the research repository.

04 - Continuous Testing

Always-on research loops

Features tagged to research themes and continuously tested - not in one-off research rounds, but as a living loop. New design decisions automatically connected to the evidence base, with gaps flagged as they open.

05 - Synthesis Refinement

Training Claude on Etch's research patterns

As Claude is exposed to more Etch research cycles, its synthesis output will become more calibrated to the kinds of insights Etch research consistently surfaces. Iterating on prompts and synthesis approaches to close the gap between what Claude produces and what researchers need.

06 - Shareable Synthetic Users

Extending synthetic users to EQ product teams

Synthetic users built from Etch's research data could be made available to the wider EQ product team - giving product managers a fast, grounded tool for hypothesis testing that doesn't require a new research cycle to be commissioned.

The Principle

AI accelerates.
Humans decide.
Always.

Claude has made Etch research faster, more structured, and more scalable. It has not made it easier to know what matters. That remains the job of researchers who were in the room, who read the silences, who understood the stakes. The combination - not either alone - is what makes this model work.
